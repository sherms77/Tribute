THE HISTORY OF HIP HOP
This is a brief history of Hip hop culture.
It oulines its origins and shows a timeline of major milestones in its history.

ABOUT

--Origin--
Hip hop is a cultrue and art movement that was created by African Amercians, Latino Americans and Caribbean Americans.
It originated in the Bronx, New York City.

It emerged in the 1970s from neighborhood block parties thrown by the Black Spades. 
The Black Spades are an African American group that has been described as being a gang, a club and a music group.

--Elements--
Hip hop is often thought of as a just a genre of music but it consists of a few different elements. 
There are four key elements which are:
- 1.Rapping or MCing,
- 2.Djing,
- 3.Breakdancing, and
- 4.Graffiti.

Other elements include:
- knowledge about hip hop culture,
- beatboxing,
- entrepreneurship
- hip hop language, and
- hip hop fashion and style.

--Influences--
Hip hop culture was largely driven by its music. 
However its musical and cultural influences is what helped shape its other elements like breakdancing, graffiti, fashion and language.

In terms of the music, it drew from a wide variety of influences from African, Latino and Caribbean cultures. 
It also drew on American music such as soul, jazz, RnB, rock and blues.

Language, fashion and dance styles were influenced by African, Latino and Caribbean cultures.

--Key people--
Some key people and groups that influenced Hip hop culture are:
- The Black Spades
- DJ Kool Herc
- Grand Master Flash
- Afrika Bambaata
- Granmaster Kaz
- Grand Wizzard Theodore
- Melle Mell
- Lovebug Starski
- Sugar Hill Gang
- Kurtis Blow

TIMELINE
- 1970s: Underground movement known as Hip hop began to form in the Bronx, New York City.
- 1980s: Kurtis Blow released his self-titled debut album which featured the single "The Breaks." 
         It became the first certified gold rap song. It helped spearhead Hip hop music's migration into the mainstream.
- 1990s: Saw the emergence and success of 'gangsta rap.'
- 2000s to present: Hip hop culture is now a dominant global force in the areas of music, movies, television, fashion, language and a number of other businesses and fields.
